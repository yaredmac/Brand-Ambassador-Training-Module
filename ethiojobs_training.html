The Pitch Framework

Module 3 of 5 · Approx. 20 min

P

PROBE — Ask First, Talk Second

Understand their situation before presenting anything. The more you know about their pain, the better your recommendation.

"Before I tell you about us — may I ask, how do you currently find candidates when you are hiring? And what is the biggest challenge you face in that process?"
Listen for: slow hiring, too many unqualified CVs, HR overload, confidential searches, payroll headaches. These are your entry points.
I

INTRODUCE — Lead with Credibility

Keep it short. One powerful fact beats a long explanation. Never read off a brochure.

"Ethiojobs has been operating since 1998 — we are Ethiopia's number one employment platform with over a million registered candidates. Thousands of organisations trust us, from small businesses to large banks and NGOs."
Deliver this confidently and from memory. It must sound natural, not rehearsed.
T

TAILOR — Match the Solution to Their Pain

Based on what they told you, present ONE service. Never pitch everything at once — it creates confusion and loses the sale.

Too many applicants? → Longlisting. Senior hire? → Executive Search. HR/payroll headaches? → Premium Jobs. Regular hiring? → The Platform.
One problem = one solution. Always.
C

CLOSE — Ask for the Next Step

Never leave a meeting without a clear next action. Always ask for a decision or a commitment to the next step.

"Based on what you have shared, I believe [Service Name] would save your team significant time and cost. Can we schedule a 30-minute call this week so I can send you a formal proposal?"
Never ask "Do you want it?" Instead ask "When works for you?" — it assumes forward momentum.
H

HANDLE — Respond to Hesitation Calmly

Expect objections in every meeting. They are normal — not rejections. Treat them as questions that need answers.

"That is a fair point. Many companies feel the same way before they work with us. Can I share what we did for a similar company in your industry?"
See Module 4 for word-for-word objection responses.

Handling Objections

Module 4 of 5 · Approx. 20 min

Use the responses below — adapt them naturally, never read them word for word. Click each objection to reveal the response.

"We already use another platform / we post on Telegram."
+
That is great — many of our clients use multiple channels too. The difference is reach and quality. Ethiojobs has over 1 million registered candidates and 30,000 daily visitors, which means when you post a job, you get significantly more qualified applicants than a Telegram post will ever reach. Would you be open to running one position through us to compare results?
"It is too expensive."
+
I completely understand — budget matters. Let me ask: how much time does your HR team spend screening CVs right now? What is the cost of a wrong hire? Our service often pays for itself in the first hiring cycle. Can I show you what companies your size typically invest and what they get back?
"We are not hiring right now."
+
Totally understand — and that is actually a great time to learn about us, with no pressure. When you do need to hire fast, you will already know exactly who to call. Can I leave you with our brochure and connect on LinkedIn?
"We handle recruitment internally."
+
That makes sense — and many companies do. Even strong internal teams use us for hard-to-fill senior roles (Executive Search) or high-volume overflow hiring. Can I share how that works?
"I need to talk to my manager."
+
Of course — that is completely normal. To make it easy, can I prepare a short one-page proposal you can share with them? It will have everything they need to decide. What is the best email to send that to?
"I have not heard of Ethiojobs / I do not trust it."
+
That is fair — let me share something quickly. Ethiojobs has been operating since 1998, over 25 years, under Infomind Solutions PLC. We are trusted by thousands of organisations in Ethiopia, including banks, NGOs, and government institutions. Would client references help build confidence?
